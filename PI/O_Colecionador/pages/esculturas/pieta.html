Title of the work:

Pietà

Artist's name:

Michelangelo

Year of the work:

1498

Description:

The Pietà is one of Michelangelo's most famous works, created in 1498-1499, when the artist was just 24 years old. The sculpture was carved from white Carrara marble and represents the Virgin Mary holding the body of Jesus Christ after the crucifixion.

Main technique:

The technique used by Michelangelo to create the Pietà was marble carving, a skill in which he was exceptionally talented. The sculpture is known for its beauty and delicacy, especially in the representation of the bodies of Mary and Jesus.
